Overview

Clinicians can be asked to provide medical evidence on an SR1 form to support a benefit claim made under the Special Rules.

The Special Rules allow people nearing the end of life to:

  • get faster, easier access to certain benefits
  • get higher payments for certain benefits
  • avoid a medical assessment

Use this service to tell the Department of Work and Pensions (DWP) that you have a patient who:

  • has a progressive disease and
  • you would not be surprised if they were to live for less than 12 months

Who can send an SR1 form

To send an SR1 form, you must be a:

  • GP
  • hospital consultant
  • specialist doctor
  • hospice doctor
  • senior specialist nurse (such as a clinical nurse specialist, advanced nurse practitioner or similar)

If you are a GP or GMC registered consultant, you can claim a fee for completing an SR1 form, which will be paid into your bank or building society account.

What you will need

The SR1 form asks for medical evidence of:

  • diagnosis and other relevant conditions
  • date of diagnosis
  • date that they have met the criteria under Special Rules
  • clinical features that inicare a severe progressive condition
  • treatment plan

You do not need to give a specific prognosis. Please use language that you would normally use when communicating with other clinicians.

Send an SR1 form

You can return the SR1 form online, by email or by post.

Send online

The digital send an SR1 form service allows you to complete the form online. This means the form arrives instantly, reducing the patient's waiting time.

The online service is currently only available to NHS England.

If you are registered with NHS Scotland, NHS Wales or Health and Social Care in Northern Ireland, you can still send an SR1 form by email or send it by post.

Start now

Send by email

Request an electronic version of the SR1 form and the fee form using a secure email address.

Secure email addresses end in:

  • @nhs.net
  • @nhs.uk
  • @nhs.scot
  • @nhs.wales.uk
  • @gov.uk

Do not encrypt any part of the email. Any email that does not come from a secure email account will be automatically deleted.

No signature is required on either form for this method of return.

Send the request for an electronic version of the SR1 form, and fee form if required, to form.e-sr1@dwp.gov.uk. with your:

  • surgery/hospital/postal address
  • phone number

Send by post

If you've completed a paper copy of the SR1 form or fee form, please sign it. Only original signed copies of these forms will be accepted by post.

There are 2 options for returning the forms to us:

  • give the SR1 form to the person who requested it and return the fee form separately to us, or
  • post the completed SR1 form and fee form directly to: [address]

When returning SR1 forms, please use a separate envelope for each patient. Do not staple documents together.

Requesting paper SR1 forms

It's easier and quicker to complete an SR1 form online but if you're unable to access the online service, we can accept paper versions.

Requests for a book of paper SR1 forms must be made on this order form for DWP information materials (opens in a new tab).

Return the form along with a cover letter on headed paper. The cover letter should include a list of clinicians who may complete SR1 forms.
